A Month in

Carmel-by-the-Sea

A Daily Itinerary · June 29 – July 28, 2026
Nature · Walking · Shopping · Museums & Aquariums · Active Days · Great Tables

Everything in the Carmel sections sits within about a 45-minute drive of town — up the coast to Big Sur, down to Garrapata, across to Monterey and Pacific Grove, and inland to Carmel Valley. The month alternates big nature/active days with relaxed village, shopping, and museum days, and spreads the marquee restaurants out so nothing feels rushed. Two fixed pieces are built in: the Pebble Beach golf day (Jul 3) and a four-day Napa Valley trip (Jul 5–8) around the Auberge du Soleil and Shafer reservations. Tap any place name for directions, hours, and photos.

A few planning notes

  • Reserve the top dinners early. Aubergine, Chez Noir, Foray, Sierra Mar, and Pacific’s Edge book out — lock these in as soon as you arrive (or before).
  • Arrive early at Point Lobos and Pfeiffer Beach — both close their gates when full, often well before mid-morning.
  • Booked commitments: golf at The Hay 11:20am + The Bench lunch 1:15pm (Jul 3); Auberge du Soleil dinner 6:15pm (Jul 6); Shafer tasting 1–3pm (Jul 7). Everything else flexes around these.
  • Monday dinners (e.g. Jul 20): Aubergine, Foray, and Chez Noir are closed Mon–Tue. For a standout Monday meal, Sierra Mar is open daily, with Casanova and Anton & Michel as close-to-home options.
  • The coast is foggy mornings, clearing afternoons; Carmel Valley is reliably warmer and sunnier when you want heat.

Days 1–6 · Carmel Essentials + Pebble Beach Golf

Mon, Jun 29Settle in & get your bearings
Morning
Arrival & unpack Ease in. Stretch your legs on the Carmel Beach Walk — a gorgeous, easy stretch of white sand and bluff-top path right in town.

Carmel Belle Counter-service cafe tucked in a courtyard off San Carlos; fresh sandwiches, salads, good coffee. Casual first-day pick.
Afternoon
Wander the village Stroll Ocean Avenue and the hidden courtyards. Carmel-by-the-Sea has no street addresses and is built for browsing — galleries, boutiques, fairy-tale cottages.
Dinner
La Bicyclette Cozy French-Italian on Dolores; wood-fired, market-driven, European feel. A perfect low-key welcome dinner. Reserve.
Tue, Jun 30The crown jewel: Point Lobos
Morning
Point Lobos State Natural Reserve Arrive by 8–9am before the lot fills. Walk the Cypress Grove + Sea Lion Point loops — turquoise coves, otters, sea lions, often whales. Rated the single best thing in the area.
Lunch
Picnic or back to town Bring a picnic (no food sold inside), or head back into Carmel.
Afternoon
Rest / beach time Easy afternoon back at Carmel Beach or your place after the morning hike.
Dinner
Treehouse Cafe Relaxed Mediterranean (Greek/Italian/Thai notes), great patio, dog-friendly. Try the falafel and sea bass.
Wed, Jul 1Monterey Bay Aquarium day
Morning
Monterey Bay Aquarium World-class. Book timed tickets online. The kelp forest, jellies, and otter feedings are the highlights — give it 3–4 hours.
Lunch
Inside the aquarium or on Cannery Row Aquarium food is pricey; alternatively walk out to Cannery Row.
Afternoon
Cannery Row stroll + shopping Steinbeck Plaza, Monterey Canning Co. shops, and the waterfront Rec Trail. Steinbeck history all around.
Dinner
The Sardine Factory A Monterey institution since 1968 — abalone bisque, classic seafood and steaks in the Conservatory room. Reserve.
Thu, Jul 2Garrapata bluffs & coast
Morning
Garrapata State Park ~15 min south on Hwy 1. Coastal bluff trails, wildflowers, sea otters, and the climb up Whale Peak. Less crowded than Point Lobos, no entry fee.
Lunch
From Scratch (The Barnyard) Guy Fieri-featured brunch/lunch spot in the Barnyard center — huevos rancheros, crab cake Benedict. Browse the Barnyard shops after.
Afternoon
Barnyard & Crossroads shopping Garden-set shopping centers just east of town — bookstore, boutiques, tasting rooms.
Dinner
Chez Noir Michelin-starred, intimate, seafood-forward New American on 5th. One of Carmel's best tables — reserve well ahead. (Open Thu.)
Fri, Jul 3Golf at Pebble Beach — booked
11:20 AM

1:15 PM
Lunch — The Bench Your reservation at The Bench at The Lodge, overlooking the 18th green and Stillwater Cove. Wood-fired, open-kitchen menu with a knockout view. Booked.
Afternoon
Linger at Pebble Beach After lunch, walk the Lodge grounds and the 18th-hole coastline, or browse the Pebble Beach shops before heading back.
Dinner
Light dinner in Carmel Keep it easy after a full golf day — a relaxed village table (Treehouse, Portabella, or Nora’s).
Sat, Jul 4Independence Day, low-key coast
Morning
Asilomar State Beach Pacific Grove. Boardwalk through the dunes, tide pools, turquoise water — easy, beautiful walking. Go early to beat the holiday crowd.
Lunch
Casual in Pacific Grove Grab lunch in downtown PG before the holiday gets busy.
Afternoon
Beach / relax Keep it mellow on a busy holiday — enjoy Carmel Beach for sunset, a local July 4 tradition.

Mission Ranch Clint Eastwood’s historic ranch restaurant — sheep pastures, ocean views, piano bar, classic American. Iconic sunset spot. Reserve.

St. Helena Lunch in St. Helena, the valley’s prettiest main street — boutiques and cafes for an easy midday.
Afternoon
Sequoia Grove or a St. Helena tasting An afternoon tasting under the sequoias (closed Tue, open Mon), or a winery of your choice. Keep it relaxed before dinner.
6:15 PM
Dinner — Auberge du Soleil Your reservation. Michelin-starred dining on the famous terrace above the valley — arrive a little early for the sunset view over the vines. Booked.
Tue, Jul 7NAPA — Balloon + Shafer tasting
Sunrise
Hot-air balloon over the valley (optional) If you want the iconic Napa morning: a sunrise balloon flight (Napa Valley Aloft or Balloons Above the Valley) lifts off at dawn and is back mid-morning — comfortably before your afternoon tasting. Book ahead; weather-dependent.
Late morning
Easy brunch Light brunch and coffee — Oxbow Public Market in downtown Napa has dozens of stalls if you head south, or keep it close to the hotel.
1:00–3:00 PM
Tasting — Shafer Vineyards Your reservation in Stags Leap District — famed for Hillside Select Cabernet. Seated, in-depth tasting. Booked.
Dinner
Yountville Yountville is the valley’s dining heart, ~15 min from Shafer. Bistro Jeanty (classic French), Bottega (Michael Chiarello-founded Italian), or RH Restaurant (stunning conservatory) are all excellent. Reserve.
Wed, Jul 8NAPA — Last morning & drive back
Morning
Calistoga spa or final tasting Squeeze in a Calistoga mineral soak/mud bath (the town’s signature), or one last winery before checkout.
Midday
Drive Napa → Carmel Head back down to the coast (~2.5–3 hrs). Optional stop at Oxbow Public Market for lunch on the way out of Napa.
Dinner
Back in Carmel — Portabella Welcome-back dinner in the village. Romantic patio, Mediterranean seafood and pasta. Reserve.
